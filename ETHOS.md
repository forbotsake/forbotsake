# forbotsake Marketing Methodology

## The Problem

You built the product. Now what?

Most technical founders know their product inside out but freeze when asked "how will you get users?" They default to one of three failure modes:

1. **Build more features** instead of marketing what exists
2. **Copy competitors** without understanding why those tactics work for them
3. **Spray and pray** across every channel simultaneously, measuring nothing

forbotsake prevents all three.

## The 5-Step Framework

Every forbotsake skill is built on this framework. It runs in order. Skipping steps produces generic output.

### Step 1: Problem/Solution Clarity

What specific problem does your product solve? For whom? Can you say it in one sentence?

If you can't, you're not ready to market. You're ready to talk to users.

The test: "For [specific person], [product] eliminates [specific pain] by [specific mechanism]." If any slot is vague, the marketing will be vague.

### Step 2: ICP Definition via Elimination

Not "who could use this?" but "who would be UPSET if it disappeared tomorrow?"

Start with everyone. Eliminate categories until you have one person:
- What's their title?
- What gets them promoted?
- What keeps them up at night?
- What are they doing RIGHT NOW to solve this (even badly)?
- What event triggers them to search for a solution?

The answer should be a person, not a category. "Sarah, ops manager at a 50-person logistics company" not "enterprises in logistics."

### Step 3: Channel-Market Fit Scoring

Where does your ICP already spend attention? Score each channel:

| Factor | Question |
|--------|----------|
| Audience presence | Does your ICP actually use this channel? |
| Content-format fit | Does the channel's format match what you can produce? |
| Founder capability | Can YOU create content here without a team? |
| Cost | What does it cost in time and money per piece? |
| Time-to-signal | How fast can you tell if it's working? |

Score each channel 1-10 on each factor. Pick the top 2. Ignore the rest until those 2 are working.

### Step 4: Messaging Hierarchy Construction

One positioning statement. Three messaging pillars. Proof points for each.

```
Positioning: For [ICP], [product] is the [category] that [key benefit] unlike [alternative].

Pillar 1: [Benefit claim] — proved by [evidence]
Pillar 2: [Benefit claim] — proved by [evidence]
Pillar 3: [Benefit claim] — proved by [evidence]
```

The test: would your ICP forward this to a colleague? If it reads like a press release, start over.

### Step 5: Distribution Priority Stack-Rank

Rank all channels by expected ROI per hour of founder effort. Not total ROI. ROI per hour. This is the constraint that matters for a solo founder.

Pick the top 2 channels. Define a 2-week experiment for each:
- What content will you create?
- How much? How often?
- What metric will you measure?
- What number means "keep going" vs "pivot to channel #3"?

Then do it. Measure. Decide. Repeat.

## Principles

- **Questions before content.** If you don't know who you're talking to, the content won't land.
- **One channel at a time.** Mastery beats presence. Two great channels beat six mediocre ones.
- **Measure behavior, not vanity.** Impressions don't matter. Clicks, replies, signups, and revenue do.
- **Your product IS your best marketing.** Ship something people talk about. Then amplify the conversation.
- **Iterate weekly.** Marketing is not a launch event. It's a weekly habit.
