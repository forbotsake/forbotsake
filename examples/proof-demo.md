# Proof Demo: What ForBotSake Actually Produces

If the repo is doing its job, a technical founder should understand the transformation fast.

This is the simplest proof frame:

Input:
- a real repo
- a founder who can build
- a rough product story
- weak traction or fuzzy distribution

Output:
- a sharper positioning statement
- a defined ICP
- competitor contrast
- a content plan
- launch assets
- outreach drafts
- a repeatable GTM workflow instead of random posting

## Before

This is the starting state ForBotSake is built for:

- the product is real
- the README is not saying the sharpest thing
- launches feel improvised
- AI tools can generate posts, but they cannot decide the wedge for you
- the founder has motion, not a GTM system

Typical symptoms:
- "we have users but no reliable growth motion"
- "I can build fast but I still cannot explain the thing cleanly"
- "we keep posting and launching, but nothing compounds"
- "I do not know which channel deserves attention first"

## The First Run

Start here:

```bash
/forbotsake-marketing-start
```

That first pass is designed to force clarity before content.

It asks for the things most technical founders skip:
- what exactly are you selling
- who has the pain now
- what they do instead today
- where they already pay attention
- what proof supports the claim

## The Artifact Chain

A strong run should leave you with inspectable files, not just a chat transcript.

### 1. `strategy.md`
What changes:
- vague category language becomes a specific positioning statement
- "everyone" becomes one concrete buyer
- random channel ideas become a ranked channel strategy

What you get:
- positioning statement
- ICP
- channel strategy
- messaging pillars
- first move

### 2. `competitor-analysis.md`
What changes:
- reactive copy ideas become a market-aware angle
- feature dumping becomes contrast

What you get:
- message patterns competitors use
- whitespace to own
- which claims are crowded
- where your wedge is sharper

### 3. `icp-profile.md`
What changes:
- abstract audience theory becomes buyer language

What you get:
- trigger events
- how the buyer describes the pain
- communities and channels
- what they actually care about versus what founders assume

### 4. `content-calendar.md`
What changes:
- "we should post more" becomes a concrete publishing plan

What you get:
- channel-by-channel content slots
- proof ideas
- hooks
- cadence
- the purpose of each piece

### 5. `content/` and generated GTM assets
What changes:
- strategy stops being theory and turns into shipping material

What you get:
- review-ready copy
- launch-ready messaging
- outreach drafts
- reusable GTM assets built from the workflow

## Why This Is Better Than Generic AI Copy

The usual failure mode is easy to spot:
- ask AI for a launch post
- get polished words
- still have weak positioning
- still have no clue who it is really for
- still post to the wrong channel

ForBotSake starts earlier.

It is meant to help a founder decide:
- what to say
- who it is for
- why this angle wins
- what artifact to ship next
- where to distribute it first

That is the actual bottleneck.
